<h2>FAQs</h2>
<div class="spin-pulse-faqs">
        <div class="spin-pulse-faq-item">
            <div class="spin-pulse-faq-question">
                What is geometry?
                <span class="spin-pulse-faq-toggle">+</span>
            </div>
            <div class="spin-pulse-faq-answer">
                Geometry is a branch of mathematics that studies the sizes, shapes, positions, angles, and dimensions of objects in space. It originated from practical needs like surveying land and has evolved into a fundamental area of math with applications in physics, engineering, and art.
            </div>
        </div>
        <div class="spin-pulse-faq-item">
            <div class="spin-pulse-faq-question">
                What are the main branches of geometry?
                <span class="spin-pulse-faq-toggle">+</span>
            </div>
            <div class="spin-pulse-faq-answer">
                The main branches include Euclidean geometry (based on flat planes), non-Euclidean geometry (such as hyperbolic and elliptic), analytic geometry (using coordinates), differential geometry (studying curves and surfaces), and topology (focusing on properties preserved under continuous deformations).
            </div>
        </div>
        <div class="spin-pulse-faq-item">
            <div class="spin-pulse-faq-question">
                What is Euclidean geometry?
                <span class="spin-pulse-faq-toggle">+</span>
            </div>
            <div class="spin-pulse-faq-answer">
                Euclidean geometry is the study of plane and solid figures based on the axioms and theorems outlined by the ancient Greek mathematician Euclid in his work "Elements." It assumes a flat space where parallel lines never meet and the sum of angles in a triangle is always 180 degrees.
            </div>
        </div>
        <div class="spin-pulse-faq-item">
            <div class="spin-pulse-faq-question">
                What is a polygon?
                <span class="spin-pulse-faq-toggle">+</span>
            </div>
            <div class="spin-pulse-faq-answer">
                A polygon is a closed two-dimensional shape formed by a finite number of straight line segments connected end-to-end. Examples include triangles (3 sides), quadrilaterals (4 sides), pentagons (5 sides), and so on. Polygons can be regular (all sides and angles equal) or irregular.
            </div>
        </div>
        <div class="spin-pulse-faq-item">
            <div class="spin-pulse-faq-question">
                What is the Pythagorean theorem?
                <span class="spin-pulse-faq-toggle">+</span>
            </div>
            <div class="spin-pulse-faq-answer">
                The Pythagorean theorem states that in a right-angled triangle, the square of the length of the hypotenuse (the side opposite the right angle) is equal to the sum of the squares of the lengths of the other two sides. Mathematically, it is expressed as \(a^2 + b^2 = c^2\), where \(c\) is the hypotenuse.
            </div>
        </div>
        <div class="spin-pulse-faq-item">
            <div class="spin-pulse-faq-question">
                What is the difference between area and perimeter?
                <span class="spin-pulse-faq-toggle">+</span>
            </div>
            <div class="spin-pulse-faq-answer">
                Perimeter is the total length of the boundary of a two-dimensional shape, calculated by summing the lengths of all sides. Area, on the other hand, measures the space enclosed within the boundary, often using formulas specific to the shape, such as length times width for a rectangle.
            </div>
        </div>
        <div class="spin-pulse-faq-item">
            <div class="spin-pulse-faq-question">
                What are congruent figures?
                <span class="spin-pulse-faq-toggle">+</span>
            </div>
            <div class="spin-pulse-faq-answer">
                Congruent figures are geometric shapes that have the same size and shape, meaning they can be superimposed on each other exactly through rotations, reflections, or translations. Congruence is denoted by the symbol ≅ and is determined by matching corresponding sides and angles.
            </div>
        </div>
        <div class="spin-pulse-faq-item">
            <div class="spin-pulse-faq-question">
                How do you calculate the circumference of a circle?
                <span class="spin-pulse-faq-toggle">+</span>
            </div>
            <div class="spin-pulse-faq-answer">
                The circumference of a circle is the distance around its boundary. It is calculated using the formula \(C = 2\pi r\), where \(r\) is the radius of the circle, or \(C = \pi d\), where \(d\) is the diameter. \(\pi\) is approximately 3.14159.
            </div>
        </div>
        <div class="spin-pulse-faq-item">
            <div class="spin-pulse-faq-question">
                What is trigonometry?
                <span class="spin-pulse-faq-toggle">+</span>
            </div>
            <div class="spin-pulse-faq-answer">
                Trigonometry is a branch of geometry that studies the relationships between the sides and angles of triangles. It introduces functions like sine, cosine, and tangent, which are essential for solving problems in navigation, astronomy, physics, and engineering.
            </div>
        </div>
        <div class="spin-pulse-faq-item">
            <div class="spin-pulse-faq-question">
                What is coordinate geometry?
                <span class="spin-pulse-faq-toggle">+</span>
            </div>
            <div class="spin-pulse-faq-answer">
                Coordinate geometry, also known as analytic geometry, uses a coordinate system (like the Cartesian plane) to represent geometric shapes algebraically. Points are defined by coordinates (x, y), and equations describe lines, curves, and conic sections, bridging algebra and geometry.
            </div>
        </div>
